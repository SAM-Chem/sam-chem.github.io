SAM–Chem

large–eddy simulation with interactive chemistry

A fast, modular, and accessible platform to investigate atmospheric chemistry and transport at the spatial and temporal scales of individual clouds and convective plumes — far finer than traditional chemical transport models.

What SAM–Chem offers

Built on the System for Atmospheric Modeling (SAM), SAM–Chem adds a fully coupled chemistry module without sacrificing performance.

Explicit Deep Convection

Resolves convective updrafts, cloud microphysics, and lightning at ~50 m horizontal resolution. Captures plume-scale isoprene–NOx segregation that is parametrized away in coarser models.

Interactive Emissions

Surface isoprene and soil NOx emissions respond to light, temperature, and precipitation-driven soil moisture. Lightning NOx is generated in real time from simulated convective intensity and cloud-to-ground/intracloud flash rates.

KPP-Compatible Chemistry

Plug-and-play gas-phase mechanisms via KPP. Ships with the CloudChem mechanism (44 gas-phase + 7 photolysis reactions) tuned for tropical isoprene–NOx–OH chemistry. Swap in any mechanism for other applications.

Model architecture

SAM–Chem couples a large-eddy simulation dynamical core with interactive gas-phase chemistry, emissions, and deposition.

Why SAM–Chem?

Traditional chemical transport models parametrize convection away, SAM–Chem simulates it explicitly. SAM–Chem couples LES-resolution dynamics with interactive chemistry, from the surface to 20 km at ~50m resolution.

Use cases

SAM–Chem was developed with tropical deep convection in mind, but its modular design supports a range of atmospheric chemistry applications.

Tropical chemistry & convection

Simulate isoprene, NOx, OH, and related species in Amazonian or Maritime Continent environments. Constrain with ARM, ATTO, GoAmazon, or airborne campaign data.

Subgrid process studies

Quantify chemistry–turbulence segregation, canopy-layer chemistry, or lightning NOx production at resolutions inaccessible to global and regional models.

Satellite retrieval support

Generate high-resolution vertical profiles to interpret satellite retrievals (e.g., CrIS isoprene, OMI NO2) that lack vertical sensitivity information.
